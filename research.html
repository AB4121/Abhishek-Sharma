Research

Current Projects

Synchronization in Adaptive Kuramoto-Sakaguchi Networks

Status: Ongoing | Collaborators: IIT Indore Complex Systems Group

This project investigates how coupled oscillators with frequency adaptation exhibit novel synchronization patterns. We explore phase transitions, bifurcations, and the emergence of chimera states in higher-order networks.

Key Methods: Numerical simulations, bifurcation analysis, statistical methods

Applications: Neural dynamics, power grid stability, climate modeling

Higher-order Interactions and Network Dynamics

Status: Ongoing

Understanding how group interactions beyond pairs influence collective dynamics. We study simplicial complexes and hypergraphs to model real-world systems more accurately.

Key Methods: Hypergraph analysis, topological methods, simulation

Applications: Social networks, biological systems, disease spreading

Finite-size Effects in Oscillator Systems

Status: Published

Examining how finite system sizes affect synchronization transitions and critical behavior. We derive scaling laws and universal exponents for various network topologies.

Key Results: New universality classes, finite-size scaling relations

Research Interests

  • Nonlinear Dynamics: Synchronization, oscillations, bifurcations, chaos
  • Network Science: Complex networks, graph theory, community detection
  • Computational Methods: Numerical simulations, machine learning applications
  • Applications: Neural systems, power grids, climate, social systems
